Research Area

Connected and Automated Systems

This area focuses on the design and analysis of infrastructure systems that integrate connected and automated vehicles, with an emphasis on coordination, collaboration, safety, and efficiency in mixed-autonomy environments.

Explore Core Themes Back to Research

Core Focus

Infrastructure and vehicles as interacting decision-makers

Connected and automated systems create opportunities to move beyond infrastructure-only control. Vehicles can become active participants in sensing, prediction, coordination, and control.

My work studies how infrastructure controllers and connected automated vehicles can interact to improve safety and operational performance while accounting for mixed traffic, uncertainty, and deployment constraints.

Trajectory optimization

Optimization of vehicle trajectories in intersections, freeway merge segments, roundabouts, and other complex facilities.

Mixed-autonomy control

Control strategies that account for interactions between connected automated vehicles and human-driven vehicles.

Mobile controller concepts

Frameworks such as the white phase, where connected automated vehicles help guide traffic movements through infrastructure systems.

Selected Directions

Representative research directions

White-phase intersection control

Use of connected automated vehicles as mobile controllers to support more flexible intersection operations in mixed traffic.

Joint signal and trajectory control

Optimization frameworks that coordinate infrastructure decisions and vehicle trajectories.

Automation under uncertainty

Control approaches that consider uncertainty in vehicle dynamics, sensing, and real-world implementation.

Automated operations in complex facilities

Applications in freeway merges, roundabouts, intersections, corridors, and other network elements.
