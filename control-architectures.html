Research Area

Control Architectures for Infrastructure Systems

This area focuses on the design of scalable control frameworks for infrastructure systems, progressing from local infrastructure control to collaborative multi-agent systems that operate under uncertainty.

View Architecture Spectrum Back to Research

Architecture Spectrum

From local control to collaborative systems

A central theme of this work is that infrastructure control is evolving from isolated infrastructure-based decisions toward increasingly coordinated and collaborative systems involving infrastructure, vehicles, and other mobile agents.

I

Local Infrastructure Control

Isolated decision-making at individual intersections, links, or system components.

II

Distributed Infrastructure Coordination

Information exchange and decision alignment among neighboring infrastructure controllers.

III

Cross-Domain Controller Coordination

Integration of different control systems such as signals, ramp meters, speed control, and perimeter control.

IV

Collaborative Distributed Control

Joint decision-making among infrastructure and connected mobile agents, such as automated vehicles.

V

Collaborative Hierarchical Control under Uncertainty

Multi-level control architectures that integrate prediction, uncertainty, and adaptive responses across system scales.

Coordination and collaboration

In this framework, coordination refers to the alignment of decisions across infrastructure components through information exchange and shared constraints. Collaboration reflects joint decision-making among interacting agents, such as infrastructure controllers and connected automated vehicles, toward shared system objectives.

Core Ideas

What this area develops

Scalable distributed optimization

Methods that decompose large infrastructure control problems while preserving system-level performance.

Real-time decision systems

Control frameworks designed to operate within the time constraints of practical infrastructure operations.

Infrastructure-agent integration

Architectures that connect stationary infrastructure controllers with connected vehicles and other mobile agents.

Uncertainty-aware control

Approaches that account for stochastic demand, sensing limitations, and uncertainty in system dynamics.
