Research Area

Network Modeling and Traffic Operations

This area focuses on modeling, analysis, and optimization of transportation and infrastructure networks to understand, predict, and improve system-level performance under varying demand, congestion, and uncertainty.

Explore Core Themes Back to Research

Core Focus

Understanding systems before controlling them

Network modeling provides the analytical foundation for evaluating infrastructure performance, diagnosing congestion mechanisms, and designing more effective operational strategies.

My work in this area uses traffic flow theory, simulation, optimization, and data-driven methods to represent complex system dynamics across intersections, corridors, and networks.

Network-level traffic flow modeling

Modeling how congestion, queues, and operational constraints propagate across transportation networks.

Traffic operations and performance analysis

Evaluating delay, throughput, reliability, capacity, safety-related measures, and system resilience.

Simulation-optimization

Combining mathematical models and simulation environments to test operational strategies before deployment.

Selected Directions

Representative research directions

System optimal dynamic traffic assignment

Distributed and scalable methods for modeling and optimizing time-dependent network flows.

Cell transmission model-based analysis

Network loading and control models that capture queue formation, spillback, and congestion propagation.

Operational strategies under congestion

Modeling and evaluating strategies such as traffic metering, network regulation, and integrated corridor operations.

Capacity and performance in emerging systems

Analysis of how connectivity, automation, and control technologies affect infrastructure performance.

Related Areas

Connect modeling to control and automation

Network modeling supports both infrastructure control architectures and connected automated system design.
