About

Background and perspective

I am a Professor in Civil, Construction, and Environmental Engineering at North Carolina State University, where I also serve as Associate Head for Research and Infrastructure. My work focuses on the design of intelligent, data-driven infrastructure systems that operate effectively under uncertainty.

My research background is rooted in operations research and optimization, with a sustained focus on transportation systems as a primary application domain. Over time, this work has expanded to incorporate artificial intelligence, distributed decision-making, and human–automation interaction.

I am particularly interested in bridging the gap between methodological development and real-world deployment. This includes working with agencies and industry partners to translate research into practical systems that improve infrastructure performance.

In addition to research, I am engaged in academic leadership, focusing on building interdisciplinary initiatives and supporting the development of large-scale research programs that address emerging infrastructure challenges.

Research Vision

Toward more coordinated and intelligent infrastructure systems

Modern infrastructure systems are no longer isolated physical assets. They are interconnected, data-rich, and increasingly shaped by automation, sensing, and real-time decision-making.

My work develops scalable frameworks that integrate sensing, prediction, optimization, and control to help infrastructure systems operate safely, efficiently, and resiliently under uncertainty, including settings where infrastructure components coordinate and automated agents collaborate.

Transportation systems serve as a flagship testbed, but the underlying ideas extend to broader infrastructure, logistics, and urban systems where coordinated decisions must be made across people, physical assets, and digital intelligence.

Research

Core areas and selected work

My research integrates optimization, AI, and systems engineering to design coordinated and collaborative decision systems for infrastructure. The following areas reflect three connected directions of this work: control architectures, network modeling and traffic operations, and connected and automated systems.

Control Architectures for Infrastructure Systems

Development of scalable control frameworks, from local infrastructure control to distributed, hierarchical, and collaborative multi-agent systems operating under uncertainty.

  • Coordination among infrastructure controllers

Connected & Automated Systems

Cross-cutting methodologies

Across these areas, my work develops foundational methods in distributed optimization, model predictive control, simulation-optimization, and AI-integrated decision systems. A recurring theme is the distinction between coordination, where infrastructure components align decisions through information exchange, and collaboration, where interacting agents jointly make decisions toward shared system objectives.
